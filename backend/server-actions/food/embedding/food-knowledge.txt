Sushi originated in Southeast Asia, spreading to Japan around the 8th century. The original form was fermented fish wrapped in rice, with the rice being discarded before eating.

Pizza margherita was created in 1889 by Raffaele Esposito in Naples, Italy. The toppings represent the colors of the Italian flag: red (tomatoes), white (mozzarella), and green (basil).

Paella originated in Valencia, Spain, during the 18th century. It was originally a laborers' meal cooked over an open fire in the fields, combining rice with whatever ingredients were available.

Tacos have been a staple of Mexican cuisine since pre-Hispanic times. The original form used corn tortillas filled with small fish. The word "taco" originally referred to the gunpowder wrapped in paper used in Mexican silver mines.

Curry originated in the Indian subcontinent over 4000 years ago. The word "curry" comes from the Tamil word "kari" meaning sauce or relish for rice. British traders later popularized the term globally.

Ramen was introduced to Japan from China in the early 20th century, becoming a popular street food after World War II. The modern instant version was invented by Momofuku Ando in 1958.

Pho originated in northern Vietnam in the early 20th century, influenced by both French and Chinese culinary traditions. It became popular nationwide after the Vietnam War.

Hamburgers trace their origins to Hamburg, Germany in the 19th century, but the modern sandwich form was developed in the United States. The first hamburger bun was introduced at the 1904 St. Louis World's Fair.
